Model Comparison

(AIC for score-based comparison for inference purposes and cross validation for prediction purpose?)

getAIC = function(m){
  AIC(m) %>%
  broom::tidy() %>%
  filter(names == "AIC") %>%
    pull(x)}

variables = c("Exposures only", "Controlling for demographic variables", "Controlling for demographic and socioeconomic variables", "Controlling for demographic, socioeconomic, and health variables")
model_name = c("crude_mod_mh", "mod2_mh", "mod3_mh", "full_mod_mh")
AIC = c(getAIC(crude_mod_mh), getAIC(mod2_mh), getAIC(mod3_mh), getAIC(full_mod_mh))
df = data.frame(model_name, AIC, variables)

df %>%
  knitr::kable()
model_name AIC variables
crude_mod_mh 269586.8 Exposures only
mod2_mh 251648.9 Controlling for demographic variables
mod3_mh 189028.7 Controlling for demographic and socioeconomic variables
full_mod_mh 178201.3 Controlling for demographic, socioeconomic, and health variables

From the results of models comparison, we can conclude that the full model (model controlling for demographic, socioeconomic, and health variables) is the best model.
